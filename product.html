The Story Behind the Spatula

Every spatula in our collection begins its journey in the sun-drenched olive groves of Tunisia, where ancient trees have been carefully tended for generations. Our master artisans select only the finest pieces of olive wood, looking for the perfect balance of density, grain pattern, and natural beauty.

The carving process is a meditation in craftsmanship. Using traditional hand tools passed down through five generations, each spatula takes shape over several days. The artisan works with the wood's natural grain, allowing the material to guide the final form while ensuring perfect functionality.

"The spatula is the most fundamental tool in any kitchen. It should feel like an extension of your hand, not just a utensil." — Maria Santos, Master Artisan

What makes our spatula truly special is the wood itself. Olive wood contains natural oils that make it naturally antimicrobial and food-safe. Over time, as you use and care for your spatula, it develops a beautiful patina that tells the story of countless meals prepared and shared.

Caring for Your Olive Wood Spatula

Hand Wash Only

Gently wash with mild soap and warm water immediately after use. Avoid soaking or submerging in water.

Dry Thoroughly

Pat dry with a soft cloth and allow to air dry completely before storing. Never put in dishwasher.

Monthly Conditioning

Apply food-safe mineral oil or beeswax monthly to maintain the wood's natural luster and prevent drying.
