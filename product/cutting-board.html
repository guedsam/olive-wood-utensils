The Story Behind the Cutting Board

The cutting board is perhaps our most personal creation—it's the piece that spends the most time with you in the kitchen, witnessing the daily rhythm of meal preparation. This particular design evolved from years of collaboration with professional chefs who demanded both beauty and functionality.

Each cutting board is crafted from olive wood that has been naturally seasoned for at least five years. This extended aging process ensures the wood achieves exceptional density and stability, making it resistant to warping while maintaining the natural antimicrobial properties that make olive wood ideal for food preparation.

"A cutting board should be both a practical tool and a beautiful object. It should invite you to cook, not just serve as a surface." — Isabella Moreno, Culinary Director

The generous dimensions provide ample space for all your prep work, while the natural juice groove around the perimeter helps contain liquids. Each board develops its own character over time, the wood taking on a beautiful patina that tells the story of countless meals prepared with love and care.

Caring for Your Olive Wood Cutting Board

Hand Wash Only

Clean immediately after use with mild soap and warm water. Use a soft brush or cloth to remove food particles from the juice groove.

Dry Thoroughly

Stand the board on its edge to dry completely. Never put in dishwasher or submerge in water for extended periods.

Monthly Conditioning

Apply food-safe mineral oil or board cream monthly. Pay special attention to the cutting surface and juice groove areas.
