The Story Behind the Rolling Pin

The rolling pin is where science meets art in the kitchen. What appears simple requires perfect balance, precise weighting, and a deep understanding of how dough responds to different rolling techniques. Our rolling pins have been refined over decades of collaboration with professional bakers.

Our rolling pins are carved from olive wood that has been naturally seasoned for at least five years. This extended aging process creates the ideal density and stability needed for consistent rolling performance, ensuring your dough maintains its integrity from center to edge.

"A great rolling pin should disappear in your hands, letting you focus entirely on the dough and the magic happening beneath it." — Sophia Chen, Master Baker

The smooth, perfectly cylindrical surface provides even pressure distribution, while the balanced weight allows for effortless control. Each rolling pin develops its own character over time, the wood taking on a beautiful patina that tells the story of countless pastries and breads.

Caring for Your Olive Wood Rolling Pin

Hand Wash Only

Gently wash with mild soap and warm water immediately after use. Clean the entire surface thoroughly to prevent dough buildup.

Dry Thoroughly

Pat dry with a soft cloth and allow to air dry completely before storing. Never put in dishwasher or soak in water.

Monthly Conditioning

Apply food-safe mineral oil monthly to maintain the wood's natural luster and prevent drying, especially on the rolling surface.
