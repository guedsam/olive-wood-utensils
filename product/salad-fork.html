The Story Behind the Salad Fork

The salad fork represents a unique challenge in utensil design—delicate enough to handle tender greens without damage, yet strong enough to toss hearty ingredients. Our salad forks have evolved through collaboration with professional chefs who understand the nuances of salad preparation.

Our salad forks are carved from olive wood that has been naturally seasoned for at least four years. This aging process creates the perfect density for the slender tines that can gently lift and toss without tearing delicate leaves or crushing fresh ingredients.

"A salad fork should enhance the natural beauty of fresh ingredients, never overpowering them. It's about respect for the food and the hands that grew it." — Marco Bianchi, Culinary Gardener

The elongated handle provides perfect leverage for tossing large bowls, while the carefully spaced tines ensure even distribution of dressings. Each salad fork develops its own character over time, the wood taking on a beautiful patina that tells the story of countless fresh, vibrant meals.

Caring for Your Olive Wood Salad Fork

Hand Wash Only

Gently wash with mild soap and warm water immediately after use. Pay special attention to the tines where food particles collect.

Dry Thoroughly

Pat dry with a soft cloth and allow to air dry completely before storing. Never put in dishwasher or soak in water.

Monthly Conditioning

Apply food-safe mineral oil monthly to maintain the wood's natural luster and prevent drying, especially on the tine tips.
