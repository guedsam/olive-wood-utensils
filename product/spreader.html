The Story Behind the Spreader

The spreader holds a special place in our collection—it's often the piece that introduces someone to the elegance of olive wood utensils. This seemingly simple tool has been refined over generations to achieve the perfect balance of form and function.

Our spreaders are carved from olive wood that has been naturally seasoned for at least three years. This aging process creates the ideal density for the curved blade that glides smoothly across bread, crackers, or toast without tearing or crumbling.

"A spreader should make every meal feel like a celebration. It's not just about butter—it's about bringing beauty to the everyday." — Elena Rodriguez, Master Artisan

The ergonomic handle provides comfortable control for precise spreading, while the curved blade ensures even distribution. Each spreader develops its own character over time, the wood taking on a beautiful patina that tells the story of countless breakfasts and gatherings.

Caring for Your Olive Wood Spreader

Hand Wash Only

Gently wash with mild soap and warm water immediately after use. The curved blade requires special attention to prevent buildup.

Dry Thoroughly

Pat dry with a soft cloth and allow to air dry completely before storing. Never put in dishwasher or soak in water.

Monthly Conditioning

Apply food-safe mineral oil monthly to maintain the wood's natural luster and prevent drying, especially on the blade surface.
